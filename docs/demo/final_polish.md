### Why these changes

| Original bullet | Why it was hard | Fix |
|---|---|---|
| "Efficiently schedule compute jobs that don't risk bandwidth for neighborhoods…" | "bandwidth" isn't grid vocabulary; long sentence | Critical loads first. Compute defers when neighborhoods need power |
| "Agents continuously speaking as it takes grid signal features such as (regional load forecast, transmission congestion, outage schedule) into consideration" | parenthetical mid-sentence kills reading flow; awkward grammar | Live negotiation. Agents read load forecasts, congestion, and outages in real time |
| "Leverage 'Virtual Power Plants', a network of decentralized small scale energy resources, which allow homeowners to receive compensation for allowing others to use their unused compute" | factually off — VPPs share **energy** (batteries, EVs), not compute; long subordinate clauses | Virtual Power Plants. Homeowners earn revenue by sharing energy with the grid |

### ⚠️ Heads-up on bullet 3

Original bullet says VPPs let homeowners share "unused compute." That's wrong — VPPs share **energy** (batteries, EVs, smart thermostats), not personal computers. A judge with grid knowledge will catch this. The fix above corrects it.
